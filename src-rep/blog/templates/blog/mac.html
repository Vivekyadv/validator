What is MAC address?

A media access control address (MAC address) is a unique identifier assigned to a network interface controller (NIC) for use as a network address in communications within a network segment. As typically represented, MAC addresses are recognizable as six groups of two hexadecimal digits, separated by hyphens or colons.
First 6-digits (say 00:A0:CC) of MAC Address identifies the manufacturer, called as OUI (Organizational Unique Identifier). IEEE Registration Authority Committee assign these MAC prefixes to its registered vendors.

Here are some OUI of well known manufacturers :
CC:46:D6 - Cisco
3C:5A:B4 - Google, Inc.
3C:D9:2B - Hewlett Packard
00:9A:CD - HUAWEI TECHNOLOGIES CO.,LTD

Format of MAC address?

MAC Address is 48-bits hardware number of a computer, which is embedded into network card (NIC). It has 6 groups of two hexadecimal digits (0-9, a-f). These pair of hexadecimal digits are seperated by hyphens (-) or colons (:).
